# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

We expect contributors and maintainers to interact respectfully. In
practice that means:

- Critique ideas and implementations, not people.
- Assume the other person is acting in good faith until proven
  otherwise. Ask before escalating.
- Keep discussion technical. Politics, personal attacks, and harassment
  are off-topic.
- Disagreement is fine. Repeated disregard for review feedback or
  hostile behaviour is not.

## Scope

This applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, and any communication
on project-controlled channels (including the testnet and example
deployments).

## Reporting

If you see behaviour that violates this code, contact the maintainers
listed in the repository metadata. Reports will be handled
confidentially.

Maintainers can warn, mute, or remove contributors who repeatedly
ignore this code. Banning is the last resort and is reserved for
behaviour that meaningfully harms the project or its participants.

## Why this exists

A reference implementation is only useful if outside builders feel
they can engage with it. The point of writing this down is to set a
predictable bar; the rest is judgment.
